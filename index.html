Written Works

"The Prison Creative Arts Project gives direct access to the writing of PVCC students who are completing an associate degree in General Studies while at the same time being incarcerated in Virginia’s prisons. Our perspective on the lives, experiences, and backgrounds of the incarcerated traditionally has been informed and shaped, more or less exclusively, by information derived from popular culture—from TV and movies. However, the essays, poems, stories, and other writing made available here - written in PVCC college classes by students incarcerated in the Fluvanna Correctional Center for Women, the Buckingham Correctional Center, or the Dillwyn Correctional Center - gives us unfiltered, first-hand access to the truth about these people’s - these students’ - unique lives, people who otherwise have been hidden from view, out of sight and out of mind. We thank Virginia Humanities for the grant that has made it possible to present this writing to the general public."

-Ben Sloan, Project Coordinator & Writing Instructor
